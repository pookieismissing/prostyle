Quality, Excellence & Professionalism — Makes the Difference — Since 1996

Octavious McAuley

Owner, ProStyle Haircutting Services

Octavious McAuley has been cutting hair in New Haven since 1996. ProStyle Haircutting Services started on State Street and it's still on State Street, same chair, same standard, same man behind the clippers. Thirty years in one location is rare for any business. For a Black-owned barbershop in downtown New Haven, it speaks for itself.

ProStyle is a one-chair shop. Octavious is the only barber. When you book an appointment, you're booking with him directly. There's no rotation, no guesswork about who's going to cut your hair. That's by design. Every fade, every lineup, every beard trim gets the same level of focus and the same attention to detail, because there's only one person responsible for the work and he takes that seriously.

The shop runs on what Octavious calls QE&P: Quality, Excellence, and Professionalism. You'll see it on the newsletter, on the walls, and in how the shop operates. It's not marketing language. It's the reason clients drive across town, the reason people who moved away still come back when they visit, and the reason the shop holds a 4.9-star rating on Google with over 70 reviews. The work is consistent. The environment is clean and relaxed. And when you sit down, Octavious listens to what you want before he picks up the clippers.

ProStyle was featured on WTNH in a segment with Keith Kountz. But the real advertising has always been word of mouth. People come because someone they trust told them to. And they come back because the cut was right.
